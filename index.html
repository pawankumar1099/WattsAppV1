Getting Started

Welcome to the Energy Management Dashboard! This comprehensive system helps you monitor and control your energy consumption and generation.

First Steps

  1. Dashboard Overview: The main dashboard provides real-time energy flow visualization and key metrics.
  2. Navigation: Use the sidebar to access different sections of the system.
  3. Real-time Updates: Data refreshes automatically every 5 seconds by default.

Key Features

  • Real-time energy flow monitoring
  • Battery status and health tracking
  • Grid connection monitoring
  • Household usage breakdown
  • Automated alerts and notifications
  • Device control and automation
  • Comprehensive reporting and analytics

Dashboard Guide

Energy Flow Visualization

The top section shows real-time energy flow from generation sources (solar/wind) through storage to household consumption.

Chart Controls

Use the filter buttons (24H, 7D, 30D) to change the time period displayed in charts.

Battery Status

Monitor battery percentage, health, voltage, and temperature. Color coding indicates status:

  • Green: Good (>50%)
  • Orange: Medium (20-50%)
  • Red: Low (<20%)

Grid Status

Shows whether the grid connection is active and the current load percentage.

Setting Up Alerts

Alert Types

  • Critical: Immediate attention required (system failures, emergency stops)
  • Warning: Potential issues (low battery, high grid load)
  • Info: General notifications (maintenance reminders, updates)

Configuration

Customize alert thresholds in the Alerts & Notifications section:

  1. Go to Alerts & Notifications
  2. Click "Settings" button
  3. Adjust thresholds for battery and grid load
  4. Enable/disable push notifications and email alerts

Device Control

System Controls

The Control Panel allows you to manage system settings:

  • Grid Connection: Enable/disable grid connectivity
  • Auto Load Balancing: Automatic power distribution
  • Battery Charging: Control battery charging behavior

Device Management

Individual devices can be controlled remotely:

  • Turn devices on/off
  • Set priority levels
  • Schedule automatic operations

Energy Limits

Set maximum power import limits and battery discharge thresholds for optimal efficiency.

Understanding Reports

Performance Metrics

  • Energy Efficiency: Ratio of energy consumed to energy generated
  • Cost Savings: Financial savings compared to grid-only consumption
  • CO2 Reduction: Environmental impact of renewable energy usage

Trend Analysis

Charts show patterns over different time periods to help optimize energy usage.

Peak Usage Analysis

Identify peak consumption times to optimize load scheduling and reduce costs.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Dashboard Not Updating

  • Check internet connection
  • Refresh the page
  • Verify system communication

Low Battery Alerts

  • Check solar/wind generation
  • Reduce non-essential loads
  • Enable grid charging if available

Grid Connection Issues

  • Verify grid connection status
  • Check for utility outages
  • Contact utility provider if needed

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often does the system update data?

A: By default, data updates every 5 seconds. You can change this in Settings.

Q: Can I control devices remotely?

A: Yes, the Control Panel allows remote device management and scheduling.

Q: How is energy efficiency calculated?

A: Efficiency is the ratio of renewable energy used to total consumption, expressed as a percentage.

Q: What happens during a power outage?

A: The system automatically switches to battery power for essential loads based on your priority settings.

Q: How can I reduce my energy consumption?

A: Use the Reports section to identify peak usage times and adjust device schedules accordingly.
